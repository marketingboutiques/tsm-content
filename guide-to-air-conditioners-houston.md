# A Guide to Air Conditioners for Your Houston Home

Living in Houston, Texas means dealing with long, hot summers. A good air conditioner is very important. The high heat and humidity make your AC work hard. If you don't pick the right one, it might break down. 

Before we look at all the different AC choices, let's answer the top three questions we hear from Houston homeowners.

## 1. What is the most efficient air conditioner for a Houston home?

The most efficient choice is a **variable-speed central air conditioner**. 

* Old AC units turn all the way on, and then all the way off. This uses a lot of energy.
* Variable-speed units run longer but at slower speeds.
* Running longer helps pull heavy humidity out of the Houston air.
* Look for an energy rating (called SEER2) of 16 to 18 or higher.

## 2. What is the difference between central AC and a mini-split in Houston?

The main difference is the air ducts. 

* **Central AC:** Uses hidden air ducts in your attic to blow cool air into every room at once.
* **Mini-Split:** Has no ducts. It uses small lines that connect to a box on your wall. This cools just one room at a time.

Central AC is best to cool a whole house. But, if you live in an old Heights bungalow without ducts, a mini-split is much better.

## 3. How much does a new central AC cost in Houston?

A new central AC in Houston usually costs between **$6,300 and $7,500**. This is for a normal 2,000-square-foot home. 

Your true cost depends on:
* The size of your house.
* The energy rating (SEER2) of the unit.
* If your air ducts need to be fixed first.

## The Different Types of Air Conditioners

As the world gets hotter, we need AC more than ever. But there are too many options. This can make it hard to choose. Read below to learn the good and bad points of each AC type.

### Central Air Conditioners

Central AC is the classic choice for big homes. It uses air ducts. It blows cool air into the rooms and sucks warm air back out to cool it again.

**Pros:**
* **Cool the whole house:** It keeps every room cold.
* **Looks nice:** The parts are hidden outside or in the attic.
* **Quiet inside:** The loud motor stays outside in the yard.

**Cons:**
* **High price:** It costs a lot to buy and set up.
* **Needs ductwork:** If you do not have air ducts, adding them is very expensive.
* **Duct cleaning:** You must clean the ducts to stop mold.

### Window Air Conditioners

These are single boxes built to cool just one room. They fit right into a window.

**Pros:**
* **Cheap:** They are the lowest cost option.
* **Easy setup:** You can put it in the window yourself.
* **Moveable:** Great for renters because you can take it when you move.

**Cons:**
* **Weak cooling:** They can only cool small spaces.
* **Blocks the view:** They block your window and block the sunlight.

### Ductless Mini-Split Systems

These systems use an outdoor motor and an indoor wall unit. They do not use air ducts. 

**Pros:**
* **Saves energy:** Air does not get lost sliding through hot attic ducts.
* **Cool one room:** You can turn them off in empty rooms to save money.

**Cons:**
* **Cost:** Buying them for multiple rooms gets expensive quickly.
* **Wall space:** You must hang a big plastic box on your wall.

### Portable Air Conditioners

These are tall boxes that sit on the floor. You can wheel them from room to room. They use a plastic tube to blow hot air out of a window.

**Pros:**
* **Very flexible:** You can roll them anywhere.
* **No tools needed:** They are very easy to set up.

**Cons:**
* **Loud noise:** The loud motor is sitting right in the room with you.
* **Not efficient:** They struggle a lot against the hot Texas summer.

### Hybrid (Dual-Fuel) Systems

A hybrid system mixes an electric heat pump with a gas heater. It automatically picks the cheapest energy source to use based on the weather outside.

**Pros:**
* **Lower bills:** They save you a lot of money on your monthly energy bill.
* **Green energy:** Using less power is better for the planet.

**Cons:**
* **High starting cost:** These smart systems cost a lot of money to install at first.

### Geothermal Systems

These systems use pipes buried out in your yard. The dirt deep underground stays the same temperature all year. The system pushes water through the dirt to cool your home.

**Pros:**
* **Amazing efficiency:** They save huge amounts of electric power.
* **Long life:** The underground pipes can last for many, many years.

**Cons:**
* **Huge cost:** Digging up your yard to lay pipes is very expensive.
* **Needs a big yard:** You must have enough land space to bury the pipes.
