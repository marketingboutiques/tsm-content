## 1. What is the most efficient air conditioner for a Houston home?

The most efficient choice is a **variable-speed central air conditioner**. 

* Old AC units turn all the way on, and then all the way off. This uses a lot of energy.
* Variable-speed units run longer but at slower speeds.
* Running longer helps pull heavy humidity out of the Houston air.
* Look for an energy rating (called SEER2) of 16 to 18 or higher.

## 2. What is the difference between central AC and a mini-split in Houston?

The main difference is the air ducts. 

* **Central AC:** Uses hidden air ducts in your attic to blow cool air into every room at once.
* **Mini-Split:** Has no ducts. It uses small lines that connect to a box on your wall. This cools just one room at a time.

Central AC is best to cool a whole house. But, if you live in an old Heights bungalow without ducts, a mini-split is much better.

## 3. How much does a new central AC cost in Houston?

A new central AC in Houston usually costs between **$6,300 and $7,500**. This is for a normal 2,000-square-foot home. 

Your true cost depends on:
* The size of your house.
* The energy rating (SEER2) of the unit.
* If your air ducts need to be fixed first.

## Houston Rebates

Buying a good AC is expensive. But you can get help! Houston homeowners who buy high-efficiency units can often get money back. 

* Look for rebates from CenterPoint Energy. 
* You can also get federal tax credits. 
* Ask your Texas Strong Mechanical tech about these deals to save money.
