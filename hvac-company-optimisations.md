## Key Questions and Answers

### How do I know if I need HVAC repair or a full replacement?
Deciding whether to fix your unit or buy a new one depends on three main things:
*   **Age:** If your system is more than 10 to 12 years old, it is reaching the end of its life.
*   **Repair Cost:** If the repair costs more than half of what a new unit would cost, it is usually better to replace it.
*   **Performance:** If your unit breaks down often or your energy bills are very high, a new energy-efficient system will save you money in the long run.

### What are the signs of a failing HVAC system?
Watch for these warning signs that your system is struggling:
*   **Strange Noises:** Bangs, squeals, or grinding sounds mean something is broken inside.
*   **Weak Airflow:** If you barely feel air coming from the vents, the blower motor or ducts may have an issue.
*   **Bad Smells:** Musty or burning smells coming from your AC suggest mold or electrical problems.
*   **Inconsistent Temps:** If some rooms are freezing while others are hot, your system is failing to balance the air.

### How often should I schedule HVAC maintenance in Houston?
In the Houston heat, your AC works very hard. You should schedule maintenance **twice a year**:
*   **Spring Tune-Up:** Get your AC ready for the hot summer months.
*   **Fall Tune-Up:** Check your heater before the first winter cold snap.

### What is included in a routine HVAC inspection?
A professional technician will check these important parts:
*   **Cleaning Coils:** Dirt on the coils makes the unit work harder and use more power.
*   **Checking Refrigerant:** They ensure your coolant levels are right so the air stays cold.
*   **Inspecting Parts:** They look at belts, motors, and electrical connections for wear and tear.
*   **Clearing Drain Lines:** This prevents water damage and mold growth in your home.
