## Common AC Problems We Fix in Houston (Problem Cluster)

When your cooling system struggles, it's often due to specific local environmental factors. We specialize in diagnosing and resolving the most frequent Houston AC issues:

*   **AC Not Cooling:** Often caused by a tripped breaker, a clogged filter, or a failing thermostat. Our technicians quickly identify the root cause to get your home back to a comfortable temperature.
*   **AC Leaking Water:** High Houston humidity leads to heavy condensation. If your drain line is clogged or your pan is rusted, we provide rapid clearing and repair to prevent expensive water damage.
*   **AC Making Strange Noise:** Grinding, squealing, or banging sounds often point to failing blower motors or loose components. We perform precision mechanical repairs to ensure quiet and safe operation.
*   **AC Blowing Warm Air:** This is usually a sign of a refrigerant leak or a failed compressor. We use electronic leak detectors to find and fix the issue rather than just "topping it off."
*   **Thermostat Not Working:** A faulty thermostat can prevent your system from turning on or cause it to cycle incorrectly. We repair and install modern, reliable controls to optimize your comfort.

## Why AC Systems Fail More Often in Houston

Our climate is one of the harshest in the country for HVAC equipment. Understanding why systems fail helps you stay ahead of major breakdowns:

1.  **Extreme Heat:** Houston's triple-digit summers force AC units to run for 12–16 hours a day, leading to rapid component wear and electrical failure.
2.  **High Humidity:** Humidity levels of 70–90% put massive strain on your system's dehumidification capabilities, often leading to frozen coils and clogged drain lines.
3.  **System Overuse:** Unlike other regions, Houston systems rarely get a break, which accelerates the aging process of motors, capacitors, and compressors.
4.  **Poor Maintenance:** Without regular [AC Maintenance in Houston](https://texasstrongmechanical.com/air-conditioning/ac-maintenance-houston-tx/), dust and salt air (near the coast) build up on coils, causing the system to overheat and fail prematurely.
