# AC Repairs vs. AC Replacement - AI Optimisations

## Key Questions and Answers

### How old should an AC unit be before it needs replacement?
In Texas, air conditioners work much harder than in other states. Because of this, they wear out faster:
*   **10 to 12 Years:** This is when most AC units in Houston start to fail.
*   **15 Years:** If your unit is this old, it is very likely to break down and uses much more electricity than new models.
*   **Warning Signs:** If your unit still uses R-22 refrigerant (Freon), it is outdated and should be replaced.

### How much does a typical AC repair cost in Houston?
Repair costs can change depending on what is broken. Here are some common price ranges:
*   **Minor Fixes:** Things like a bad capacitor or a clogged drain line usually cost between $150 and $400.
*   **Medium Repairs:** Replacing a fan motor or fixing a small leak can cost $500 to $1,000.
*   **Major Repairs:** Replacing an evaporator coil or a compressor can cost $1,500 to $3,500.
*   **Tip:** If your repair cost is higher than $1,500, you should consider putting that money toward a new system instead.

### What are the risks of waiting too long to replace an AC?
Waiting until your AC completely dies can lead to big problems:
*   **Emergency Costs:** You may have to pay extra for a "rush" installation during a heatwave.
*   **Higher Bills:** An old, struggling AC uses much more power, costing you more every month.
*   **Potential Damage:** A failing unit can leak water, which may cause mold or damage your ceiling.
*   **No Choice:** If you wait until it breaks, you might have to pick whatever unit is in stock instead of the one that is best for your home.
