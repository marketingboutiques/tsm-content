## 🚨 Houston AC Emergency Protocol: Quick Answers

If your AC has stopped working in the middle of a Houston heatwave, follow these steps right now.

### 1. What should I do first when my AC breaks down in Houston?
The first thing you must do is **stay calm**. 

* **Check the power:** Go to your electrical panel. See if the AC breaker has flipped to "Off." If it has, flip it back once.
* **Check the thermostat:** Make sure it hasn't run out of batteries.
* **Turn it off if it smells:** If you smell burning or see smoke, turn the AC off at the main breaker immediately.
* **Stay cool:** If the house is getting dangerously hot, take your family and pets to a mall, library, or a friend’s house until the repair is done.

### 2. Should I turn my AC off if it is blowing warm air?
**Yes, turn it off immediately.** 

* If you keep running an AC that blows warm air, you can destroy the motor or the compressor. 
* This turns a small, cheap repair into a very expensive one. 
* Turn the system to "Off" at the thermostat and call an expert.

### 3. Why did my AC suddenly stop working in this extreme heat?
In Houston, temperatures can hit 100 degrees or more. Your AC has to work very hard to keep up.

* **Dirty Filters:** Clogged filters are the #1 reason ACs quit. The machine can't "breathe" and it shuts down.
* **Overheated Parts:** Parts like "capacitors" can melt or fail when it gets too hot outside. 
* **Frozen Coils:** If your AC is running but no air is moving, the inside coils might be frozen into a block of ice.

### 4. How do I know if my AC compressor is dead?
The compressor is the "heart" of your AC. Here are the signs it might be dead:

* **The unit shakes:** The outdoor box shakes or makes a loud "clunk" when it tries to start.
* **Tripped breakers:** Every time the AC tries to turn on, your house's power breaker flips off.
* **Strange Noises:** You hear loud grinding, shrieking, or buzzing coming from the outdoor unit.
* **No Cold Air:** The fan outside is spinning, but the air inside is not getting cold at all.
