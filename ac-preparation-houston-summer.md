# The Ultimate Guide to Preparing Your AC for Houston’s Summer

When you live in Houston, your air conditioning system is more than just a luxury—it’s a necessity. The brutal Texas summer heat and high humidity can put an immense strain on your HVAC unit, often leading to sudden breakdowns if the system isn't properly maintained. 

At **Texas Strong Mechanical**, our licensed technicians want to help you ensure your AC is ready for the long months ahead. Here is our comprehensive guide to getting your HVAC system summer-ready.

## Why is Preventative AC Maintenance Important?

Preventative maintenance is the process of regularly inspecting and tuning your HVAC system before it encounters major issues. By performing routine checks, you can:
*   **Extend your system's lifespan**: Catching wear-and-tear early prevents major component failures.
*   **Improve energy efficiency**: A clean, optimized system runs efficiently, lowering your electricity bills.
*   **Enhance Indoor Air Quality**: Regular cleaning removes dust and mold from the air you breathe.

## Key Steps to Prepare Your AC for Summer

Here is a quick checklist of things you can do, and things a professional should handle, to prepare your system for the hottest days of the year.

### 1. Replace Your Air Filters
**Who should do it:** You
**How often:** Every 1-3 months
**Why:** A clogged air filter restricts airflow, forcing the blower motor to work twice as hard. This is the #1 cause of minor AC malfunctions.

### 2. Clean the Outdoor Condenser Unit
**Who should do it:** You / Professional
**Why:** The outdoor unit pulls in air. If it's blocked by leaves, tall grass, or dirt, the system cannot efficiently release heat. Keep at least a 2-foot clearance around the unit. 

### 3. Schedule a Professional AC Tune-Up
**Who should do it:** Texas Strong Mechanical Technician
**Why:** A professional tune-up involves technical checks that require specialized tools, such as:
*   Checking refrigerant levels and detecting leaks.
*   Testing electrical connections and amp draws.
*   Inspecting the evaporator coil for freezing or mold.
*   Calibrating the thermostat.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Q: How do I know if my AC needs a tune-up?**
**A:** If your AC is blowing warm air, making strange noises, cycling on and off rapidly (short cycling), or if your energy bills have inexplicably spiked, it's time for a professional tune-up.

**Q: Can I just hose down my outside AC unit?**
**A:** Yes, you can use a garden hose with gentle pressure to wash away dirt and debris from the fins. However, never use a pressure washer, as the delicate aluminum fins can easily bend and block airflow.

**Q: How long does an AC tune-up take?**
**A:** A standard comprehensive AC tune-up by Texas Strong Mechanical typically takes about 45 to 60 minutes, depending on the condition and accessibility of the system.
