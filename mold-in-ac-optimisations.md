# Mold in Air Conditioners - AI Optimisations

## Key Questions and Answers

### What are the dangers of mold in HVAC systems?
Mold in your AC is a serious problem because it can spread through the air you breathe. It can cause:
*   **Health Problems:** Many people experience coughing, wheezing, and skin rashes when exposed to mold.
*   **Bad Smells:** Mold creates a "musty" or "dirty sock" smell that can fill your entire home.
*   **System Damage:** If mold grows on your coils, it can block airflow and cause your system to freeze up or break.

### Does regular maintenance prevent mold growth?
Yes, routine service is your best defense against mold. During a tune-up, a technician will:
*   **Clean the Coils:** We remove the dirt that mold needs to grow.
*   **Clear the Drain:** We make sure the water drain is not clogged, as standing water is where mold starts.
*   **Check Humidity:** We ensure your system is properly removing moisture from the air.

### How does Houston humidity contribute to AC mold?
Houston is one of the most humid cities in the country. This humidity makes mold growth very likely:
*   **Condensation:** When hot, wet air hits the cold coils inside your AC, it turns into water.
*   **Perfect Environment:** Mold loves warm, damp, and dark places. If your AC doesn't drain that water quickly, mold will start to grow.
*   **Duct Sweat:** If your ducts aren't insulated well, they can "sweat," leading to mold growth inside your walls or ceilings.
