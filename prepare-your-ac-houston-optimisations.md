## 1. What temperature should I set my AC to in Houston during the summer?

Energy experts say **78 degrees** is the best setting. 

* Sticking to 78 degrees helps save money on your electric bill.
* Do not set it super low (like 68 degrees) to cool the house faster. It will only overwork the AC.
* **Pro Tip:** Use ceiling fans. A fan will make a 78-degree room feel like it is 74 degrees.
* When you leave the house, turn the thermostat up to 80-85 degrees so you do not waste power on an empty house.

## 3. How often should I change my AC filter in Texas during the summer?

You must check and change your air filter **every 30 days** during a Houston summer.

* In the summer heat, your AC runs almost non-stop.
* Dirt builds up on the filter very fast. 
* A dirty, clogged filter makes it hard for the machine to breathe. 
* If the AC cannot breathe, it works harder, costs more money to run, and might even freeze the coils and break.

## 5. How do I know if my AC needs to be replaced before summer?

Watch out for these danger signs before the heat wave arrives:

* **Age:** Your unit is more than 10 to 15 years old.
* **High Bills:** Your electric bills are suddenly much higher than normal.
* **Bad Air Flow:** The air coming out of the vents feels weak or warm.
* **Strange Noises:** You hear banging, grinding, or squealing from the outdoor unit.
* **Constant Repairs:** You had to call for repairs several times last year. 

If you see these signs, it might be cheaper to buy an energy-efficient new unit instead of pouring money into an old, broken one.
